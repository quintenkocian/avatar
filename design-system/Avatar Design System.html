01 — Overview

Sharp, fresh, and unmistakably not a chatbot.

Avatar is a personal digital twin running on someone's website. It looks like a precise instrument, reads like an editorial product, and treats the rare moment when the real human joins as the emotional centre of the experience.

Instrument, not toy

Crisp type, a faint technical grid, monospace metadata. Confident and a little futuristic — never playful filler.

The human is the spark

Yellow is reserved for the moments the real person matters: their message, the "needs you" flag, focus.

Earned restraint

No gradients in chrome, no purple wash, no accent bars down the side of panels, no emoji. Every mark earns its place.

This document dogfoods the system: every colour, type style and component on this page is rendered from tokens.css + components.css — the same files the product ships. Toggle dark / light with the control at the bottom-right.

03 — Identity

The Avatar & the twin

Two faces, one person. The human is rendered exactly as they are — warm, photographic, real. The Avatar is the same face rebuilt as a synthetic twin: cool chrome duotone, posterised paneling, glowing eyes, a HUD frame. The contrast is the product.

The human
The real person. Used for their own messages and the admin owner chip. Always natural, never stylised. Ringed in yellow when live.
The digital twin
Derived from the same photo: cool duotone, paneling, lit eyes, corner brackets. The AI's identity throughout the chat. Ringed in cyan-blue.

Brand mark

Two discs sharing an axis — one solid (the human), one outlined with a yellow node (the twin). One identity, two states. Crisp and geometric; never illustrative.

On surfaces Avatar
The twin image is generated programmatically from the owner's photo (pic.jpg) so any site owner gets a matching synthetic twin. Treatment: cool navy→cyan duotone, ~20-band posterise, scanlines, paneling seams, eye glow, HUD brackets. Two crops ship: avatar-robot.png (square, framed) and avatar-robot-round.png (for circular chat avatars).

05 — Screens & UX

Visitor chat

A refined instant-message experience. An editorial hero, a focused composer that always holds focus, and the three-way conversation playing out in a centred column.

edwarddonner.comOpen full
  • Name & Keep chat sit quietly in the top bar. Keep-chat defaults on and restores the thread from a cookie's conversation_id.
  • Composer always wins focus — on load and after every send, by click or Enter.
  • Streaming replies via SSE, with tool use shown in small mono as it happens.
  • The human's message appears as its own bubble — the designed moment, surfaced by the 10s/60s poll.
05 — Screens & UX

Admin dashboard

An inbox the owner can move through at speed. Unread and needs-you states are unmistakable; arrow keys move between threads; Enter sends, Shift+Enter for multi-line.

edwarddonner.com/adminOpen full
  • Most recent on top. Each row: initials, name, timestamp, preview. Unread rows are brighter and carry a blue dot.
  • Needs-you threads (a push_tool fired) glow yellow and tag "Needs you" until opened.
  • Posting as you is explicit — the owner is reminded the visitor sees only their photo, no name, and the Avatar won't react.
  • Keyboard-first: ↑ ↓ between threads, Enter to send, Shift+Enter for a new line.
05 — Screens & UX

Flows & behaviour

The interaction contracts the build must honour — the parts of the experience that don't show up in a static screenshot.

Visitor — a question answered

Land & focus
Page opens, composer takes focus. If Keep-chat + a stored conversation_id exist, the prior thread is fetched and restored.
Ask
Visitor sends a message (initials bubble). A Qn shortcut (e.g. Q2) skips the model and returns the FAQ answer instantly, tagged instant · Q2.
Stream
The Avatar streams its reply over SSE. Tool calls (faq_tool, push_tool) render live in small mono, then collapse to a done state.
Re-focus
On completion the composer regains focus, ready for the next message.

Contact capture & human-in-the-loop

Intent to connect
Visitor wants to reach the human. The Avatar asks for an email and fires push_tool, telling the visitor it has notified the human.
Flag raised
The message row is marked needs_attention and unread. In admin it glows yellow and sorts to the owner's attention.
Human replies
The owner posts from admin. The message is inserted into the thread as the human role — photo, no name. The Avatar does not react to it.
Visitor sees it
The visitor's open page is polling (every 10s, easing to 60s after 5 quiet minutes) and surfaces the human's bubble — the designed moment.

05 — Screens & UX

Voice & tone

The Avatar speaks as a warm, sharp, professional twin — engaging a potential client or employer, never a generic assistant. The interface around it stays quiet and precise.

Do
  • Speak in the first person as the twin: "I built…", "Ed wrote a full curriculum".
  • Be concise and confident; use light markdown emphasis and real links.
  • When you don't know, say so and record the question for the human.
Don't
  • No "As an AI language model…", no hedging boilerplate, no emoji.
  • Don't invent answers — use the tool and defer to the human.
  • Interface copy never competes with the conversation. Labels are mono, terse, lower-key.
